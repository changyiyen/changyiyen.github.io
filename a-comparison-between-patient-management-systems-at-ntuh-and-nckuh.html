A comparison between patient management systems at NTUH and NCKUH Tweet

In May 2017 I was fortunate enough to spend the entire month at the General Medicine department at National Taiwan University Hospital. I wanted to see for myself what the hospital at the most prestigious university in Taiwan had to offer.

Besides the inter-hospital culture shock, the thing that struck me most was how different the patient management system was from the one I had become familiar with while serving as an intern at National Cheng Kung University. I admit that there must be many aspects of these systems that I do not know about, but I will attempt to list the differences between the systems and their respective pros and cons.

This table is by no means complete; feel free to let me know what I missed.

Comparison between patient management systems at NTUH and NCKUH
Technical aspects
NTUH NCKUH
Type Web application Microsoft ClickOnce application
Platform Cross-platform Microsoft Windows
Execution speed fast medium to fast
Workflow
Integration with other services Inpatient system integrated with surgery, ER, outpatient systems into unified portal page; selectable using radio button set upon login Inpatient system integrated with surgery system; segregated from ER and outpatient systems
Patient listing Listing by ward location, primary care physician, etc.
Color coding Background color change to indicate missing note Color coding to indicate patient gender
Patient privacy Text appended to patient name to indicate patient does not wish to be searchable Patient name replaced with chart number
Patient admission notes More detailed; includes herbal drug use, education of present parties, nutritional plan, etc.; no template file for review of systems and physical examination Template files for physical examination present and loadable from each account; standardized review of systems list
Patient order system
Overview Systems for medication orders, nutrition orders and diagnostic orders are segregated; miscellaneous orders grouped with diagnostic orders Medication, nutrition, diagnostic and miscellaneous orders are color coded and all grouped in one page; new orders are highlighted
Order times Hour precision Second precision
IV fluids Drip rate needs to be included in order Drip rate may be included in comment; seperate order for carrier solution needed
Long term medications brought by patient Order system is subsystem of medication system; switching from patient's own meds and ordering meds from the pharmacy is done semi-manually Orders to use patient's own medication is done via a tickbox if the pharmacy carries the drug; tickbox in miscellaneous orders is used if the pharmacy does not carry the drug
Authorization system Authorization by licensed physician is done in batch from patient's main page; all orders must be authorized Authorization by licensed physician is done once confirmation button is clicked while on medication orders page; no authorization needed for diagnostics, procedures and miscellaneous orders
